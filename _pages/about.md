I am currently a fourth year doctorate student and Ph.D. Candidate in physics at Harvard University. I earned my bachelor degrees in physics and mathematics at the University of Chicago in 2021. My work spans a heterogeneous subset of neutrino physics, from neutrino oscillations and BSM phenomenology to machine learning and hardware experiments, and I am currently focused on charm physics and related hadronic processes in neutrino telescopes. I work with Professor Carlos A. Argüelles-Delgado, and am a member of the IceCube Collaboration. I recently finished a series of neutrino oscillation-related sensitivity analysis as well as some machine learning reconstruction algorithms.

I strive to make the scientific community a better place. I engage in fun and fruitful conversations in a mandarin seminar with many other young physicists in the larger Boston Area that my friends and I started: I encourage you to check it out [here](https://longtablephysics.github.io). I also take part in maintaining an open-source neutrino simulation database [here](https://nu-mc.org): (only) half-jokingly, this is bringing fire to every neutrino theorist, phenomenologist, and experimentalist alike. Feel free to check it out!

In my free time I enjoy camping, hiking and running. I have the inspiration of qualifying for Boston Marathon before my graduation. I am also a huge fan of anime, ACG arts, and specifically Hoyoverse games including Genshin Impact, Honkai Starrail and ZZZ.
